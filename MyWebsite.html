My name is Kassondra Jones...

...but you can call me Kassie...

I was born in April of 1989 to my parents in Rochester Hills, Michigan. When I was 7 years old, we moved to the downriver area after my grandpa got sick. I spent the rest of my childhood and teen years in Woodhaven going to school, making friends, and getting my first few jobs. After graduation, I went to Wayne State University where I studied Political Science with the intention of eventually going to law school. In 2012 I married my wonderful husband, after only dating for a year and a half. While that may seem fast, we have known each other since middle school. When you know you've found the right person, you just know.

In my last semester of undergrad, I got pregnant with my oldest daughter. While she is one of the best things to have happened to me, her birth changed everything for me. Instead of moving into a graduate program at the university, I took a position in Human Resources with the hope that it would provide a positive work-life balance.

It did not.

Shortly after, I had my second child. Another beautiful, sassy little girl. Now that our family was complete, it was time that I decided what to do with my career. I pursued the Grand Circus Java bootcamp because it appeared to be the most efficient avenue to transition into a new industry.

From here, my plans are to do the following:

  1. Continue to learn as many Java functions as possible
  2. Land a job with a mid-size company and continue to grow as a developer
  3. Move into a teaching and/or mentor role for new developers
  4. Learn at least one more back-end language
  5. Learn more advanced front-end skills to earn the full-stack classification
  6. Long term goal -- to, in some way, help influence more girls/women into STEM pathways

Being a full-time mom, in addition to the bootcamp, leaves little time for hobbies and personal free time. That being said, I have always loved performance art. I love live music of all genres, theater, dance, and anything that can make an audience feel something powerful or special. Last year, a bucket-list experience was checked off when I attended a showing of Phantom of the Opera and sat on the main floor, directly beneath the falling chandelier. It's going to take a lot to top that moment, but I am always on the lookout for something that can or will!
